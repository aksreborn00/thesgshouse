Mohenjo-daro was a major city of the Indus Valley Civilization, one of the world's earliest urban centers. Built around 2500 BCE, it had advanced architecture, urban planning, and public infrastructure.

Artifacts

The city yielded incredible artifacts including the Dancing Girl statue, pottery, seals, and jewelry, showcasing the artistic and technological brilliance of the time.

History

Mohenjo-daro thrived for centuries before its mysterious decline around 1900 BCE. Its ruins provide valuable insight into the life and culture of the Indus Valley people.
